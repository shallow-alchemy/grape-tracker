## Phenology

**Budbreak Order:** Late (9 days after Chasselas). Natural frost protection.

### Harvest Timing

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Typical Harvest | Mid-September |
| Days Bloom→Harvest | 100-115 |
| Target Brix | **20-22°** |
| Target pH | 3.3-3.5 |

**Unique Characteristic:** Marsanne ripens at low sugars, often producing wines around 12% potential alcohol. This is normal for the variety.

## Blending Role

### Traditional Northern Rhône White Blends

- **Typical proportion:** **80-85%**
- **Partner:** Roussanne (15-20%)
- **Contribution:** Body, texture, honeyed character

### Single Varietal

Marsanne can make compelling single-varietal wines, particularly in Hermitage. Australian Marsanne (particularly from Tahbilk) demonstrates exceptional aging potential.

## Small-Scale Growing Notes

### Advantages

- Late budbreak reduces frost risk
- More consistent production than Roussanne
- Better disease resistance than Roussanne
- Excellent aging potential
- Lower alcohol wines (market appeal)

### Challenges

- High vigor requires constant management
- Crop thinning essential for quality
- Lower drought tolerance than other Rhône varieties
- Warm climate produces flabby, muted wines

### Beginner Accessibility

**Rating:** 2 — **Moderate-Good** (Recommended First Rhône White)

Marsanne provides more consistent production and better disease resistance than Roussanne. The primary challenge is vigor management rather than disease pressure.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Vigor:** HIGH — constant pruning and crop thinning required
2. **Sugar:** Naturally LOW — 20-22°Brix is normal; ~12% ABV typical
3. **Frost:** Late budbreak provides natural protection
4. **Disease:** High pressure but more manageable than Roussanne
5. **Drought:** Lower tolerance than other Rhône whites
6. **Blending:** Dominant partner (80-85%) in Marsanne-Roussanne blends
7. **Beginner:** Best white Rhône variety for new growers
