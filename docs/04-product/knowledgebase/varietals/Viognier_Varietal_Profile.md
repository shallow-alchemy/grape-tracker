## Phenology

**Budbreak Order:** **Earliest** (late March). Requires ~49 GDD to budbreak. High frost risk.

### Harvest Timing

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Typical Harvest | Late August–early September |
| Days Bloom→Harvest | 100-115 |
| Target Brix | **≤24°** |
| Target pH | 3.2-3.5 |

### Critical Timing: Narrow Flavor Window

Viognier's flavor development occurs within **days, not weeks**. Harvest timing separates "mean-spirited Sauvignon Blanc" from "voluptuous aromatics." This is the most critical management decision for quality.

## Winemaking Profile

### Harvest Chemistry Targets

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Brix | **≤24°** |
| pH | 3.2-3.5 |
| TA (g/L) | **7+** |

**Critical:** Prioritize flavor development over numbers. The harvest window is extremely narrow.

### Fermentation Parameters

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Temperature | **50-60°F (10-15°C)** |
| Skin Contact | None recommended |
| Oak Preference | Neutral strongly preferred |

Cool fermentation preserves delicate aromatics.

### Aging Potential

| Level | Window |
|-------|--------|
| Standard | **1-3 years** |
| Premium | 3-5 years |

**Critical:** Viognier's volatile terpenes oxidize quickly. Bottle within 4-6 months and drink young to preserve aromatics.

## Blending Role

### Syrah Co-fermentation (Côte-Rôtie Tradition)

Viognier is traditionally co-fermented with Syrah:
- **Typical Practice:** 5-10%
- **Effect:** Aromatic lift; Viognier flavanols conjugate with Syrah anthocyanins, shifting color toward purple/blue
- **Technique:** Destem and crush Viognier on top of destemmed Syrah before fermentation

### Single Varietal

Viognier makes distinctive single-varietal wines in Condrieu (Northern Rhône benchmark) and increasingly in California and Virginia.

## Small-Scale Growing Notes

### Advantages

- Distinctive aromatic profile
- Good drought tolerance
- Growing market interest
- Co-fermentation option with Syrah

### Challenges

- **Very early budbreak** — frost loss common
- High powdery mildew pressure — 10-14 day spray intervals
- **Extremely narrow harvest window** — days matter
- Short aging potential limits inventory flexibility

### Beginner Accessibility

**Rating:** 4 — Moderate-Difficult

Viognier demands precise timing at harvest and intensive disease management. Not recommended for first-time growers.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Frost:** VERY early budbreak — site selection critical
2. **Disease:** HIGH powdery mildew pressure — frequent sprays required
3. **Harvest:** NARROW window — flavor development happens in days
4. **Winemaking:** Cool ferment; neutral oak; drink young
5. **Aging:** SHORT — terpenes oxidize; bottle early
6. **Blending:** Classic co-fermentation with Syrah (5-10%)
