## Disease Susceptibility

| Disease | Rating | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Powdery Mildew | **Very High** | Primary challenge |
| Downy Mildew | Moderate | — |
| Botrytis | **High** | Tight clusters, thin skins |
| Eutypa | Standard | — |

**Nickname:** "The Princess" — highest disease pressure among Rhône whites.

### Disease Management Summary

- **Spray Intensity:** **Very High**
- **Primary Concern:** Powdery mildew
- **Secondary Concern:** Botrytis
- Full powdery mildew AND botrytis programs required

|-------|--------|
| Standard | 8-15 years |

## Blending Role

### Traditional Northern Rhône White Blends

- **Typical proportion:** 15-20%
- **Partner:** Marsanne (80-85%)
- **Contribution:** Aromatic lift, acidity, aging potential

### Single Varietal

Roussanne makes compelling single-varietal wines, particularly from old vines in sites like Hermitage and Châteauneuf-du-Pape.

## Small-Scale Growing Notes

### Advantages

- Superior aging potential
- Distinctive character
- Late budbreak reduces frost risk
- Low-moderate vigor manageable

### Challenges

- **"The Princess"** — highest disease pressure of white Rhônes
- Full powdery mildew AND botrytis programs required
- Erratic yields year to year
- Tight clusters trap moisture
- Thin skins susceptible to damage

### Beginner Accessibility

**Rating:** 5 — **Difficult**

Roussanne's disease susceptibility makes it one of the more challenging white varieties for small-scale growers. Not recommended without established spray program expertise.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Disease:** VERY HIGH pressure — "The Princess" demands intensive management
2. **Spraying:** Full powdery mildew AND botrytis programs required
3. **Yields:** Erratic — expect variability
4. **Harvest:** Watch acidity — drops quickly at end of ripening
5. **Aging:** SUPERIOR among white Rhônes — can age 15-20+ years
6. **Blending:** Provides lift/acidity to Marsanne-dominant blends (15-20%)
