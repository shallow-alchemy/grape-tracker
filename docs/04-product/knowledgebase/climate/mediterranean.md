# Mediterranean Climate Viticulture Reference Data

**Document Purpose**: AI system reference data for varietal recommendations and site assessment in California, Southern Oregon, and Mediterranean-climate regions.

---

## 1. CLIMATE CLASSIFICATION & CHARACTERISTICS

### Köppen Classification Definitions

| Classification | Name | Temperature Criteria | Precipitation Criteria |
|---------------|------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Csa** | Hot-summer Mediterranean | Coldest month >0°C (32°F); ≥1 month avg >22°C (72°F); ≥4 months >10°C | ≥3× precipitation in wettest winter month vs driest summer month; driest summer month <40mm |
| **Csb** | Warm-summer Mediterranean | Coldest month >0°C; ALL months avg <22°C; ≥4 months >10°C | Same as Csa |
| **Csc** | Cold-summer Mediterranean | Coldest month >0°C; only 1-3 months >10°C | Same as Csa |

### Regional Climate Distribution

| Climate Type | California Regions | Oregon Regions |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Csa (Hot-summer)** | Central Valley, interior Lodi, Paso Robles (east), parts of Napa/Sonoma, Sierra Foothills | Rogue Valley (Medford area) |
| **Csb (Warm-summer)** | Coastal Northern California, San Francisco Bay Area, Santa Barbara coast | Umpqua Valley, Applegate Valley, Illinois Valley |
| **BSk (Semi-arid)** | Eastern San Joaquin Valley | N/A — Washington's Columbia Valley uses this classification |

### Key Mediterranean Climate Characteristics

| Characteristic | Value/Range | Significance for Viticulture |
|---------------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Growing season rain | <5-15% of annual total | Minimal disease pressure; irrigation-dependent |
| Dry season length | 5-7 months (May-October) | Extended ripening window; drought stress management critical |
| Wet season | November-March | Soil moisture recharge; trunk disease infection window |
| Summer humidity | Low (typically 30-50% RH) | Reduced Botrytis/downy mildew; powdery mildew remains concern |

### Mediterranean vs Other Wine Climate Types

| Climate Type | Summer Rain | Winter Temps | Growing Season | Disease Pressure | Examples |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|----------------|------------------|----------|
| **Mediterranean** | Near zero | Mild (rarely <25°F) | Long (200-300+ days) | Low (except powdery mildew) | California, Southern Oregon |
| **Continental** | Moderate | Very cold (<0°F possible) | Short (150-180 days) | Moderate | Burgundy, parts of Washington |
| **Maritime** | Distributed year-round | Mild | Moderate | High (humidity) | Champagne, parts of Oregon |
| **Semi-arid** | Near zero | Cold (winter lows <10°F) | Moderate | Very low | Columbia Valley, eastern WA |

### Frost Risk Patterns by Topography

| Position | Temperature vs Valley Floor | Frost Risk Level | Mechanism |
|----------|---------------------------|------------------|-----------|
| Valley floor | Baseline (coldest) | HIGHEST | Cold air pools at lowest points |
| Frost pockets | 4-8°F colder than surroundings | EXTREME | Cold air dams behind obstacles |
| Mid-slope (thermal belt) | 2-5°F warmer | LOWEST | Cold air drains past without pooling |
| Upper hillside | 1-3°F warmer | LOW-MODERATE | Good drainage, some exposure |
| Hilltop/ridge | Variable | LOW-MODERATE | Good drainage; exposed to advective frost |

| **Diatomaceous earth** | Santa Barbara | Rare marine fossil deposits; unique minerality |
| **Calcareous** | Paso Robles, Sta. Rita Hills | 30%+ limestone; comparable to Burgundy/Champagne |

## 7. DISEASE & PEST PRESSURE

### Primary Disease Risk by Region

| Disease | Overall Risk | Conditions Favoring | Conditions Reducing |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Powdery Mildew** | HIGH (all regions) | Temps 70-85°F; fog/dew for spore release | Temps >95°F for 12+ continuous hours |
| **Downy Mildew** | LOW (Mediterranean) | Spring/summer rain; warm humid nights | Dry summers (Mediterranean advantage) |
| **Botrytis** | MODERATE (foggy areas) | >92% humidity; rain at harvest; 58-82°F | Hot dry conditions; wind |
| **Pierce's Disease** | HIGH (south); LOW (north) | Hot summers; mild winters | Cold winters (<10°F for 2-3 days) |
| **Trunk Diseases** | MODERATE-HIGH (all) | Winter rainfall; pruning wounds | Dry dormant season; late pruning |

### Disease Risk by Specific Region

| Region | Powdery Mildew | Botrytis | Downy Mildew | Pierce's Disease |
|--------|---------------|----------|--------------|------------------|
| Napa/Sonoma | HIGH | MODERATE (fog) | LOW | LOW-MODERATE |
| Central Coast | MODERATE-HIGH | MODERATE | LOW | LOW |
| San Joaquin Valley | MODERATE | LOW | LOW | MODERATE-HIGH |
| Lodi | MODERATE | LOW | LOW | MODERATE |
| Sierra Foothills | MODERATE | LOW | LOW | VERY LOW |
| Southern Oregon | MODERATE | MODERATE | LOW | VERY LOW |

### Mediterranean Climate Disease Advantages

| Disease | Why Mediterranean Climate Helps |
|---------|--------------------------------|
| Downy Mildew | Requires rain for zoospore dispersal; dry summers suppress |
| Botrytis | Needs >92% humidity + free water; dry conditions prevent |
| Black Rot | Favored by wet, humid conditions; rare in Mediterranean |
| Phomopsis | Wet spring weather needed; intermittent in Mediterranean |

### Powdery Mildew: The Dominant Mediterranean Disease

- **Annual cost to California**: $189 million in fungicides/applications
- **Accounts for**: 74% of grape pesticide use in California
- **Unique characteristic**: Thrives WITHOUT free water (unlike most fungal diseases)
- **Temperature optimum**: 70-85°F
- **Suppression**: Temps >95°F for 12+ continuous hours slow growth

### Pierce's Disease Geographic Limits

| Zone | Risk Level | Vector Status |
|------|------------|---------------|
| Southern California | HIGH | GWSS established |
| San Joaquin Valley (Kern, Tulare, Fresno) | MODERATE-HIGH | GWSS established in parts |
| North Coast (Napa, Sonoma, Mendocino) | LOW-MODERATE | Cold winters provide "curing"; native sharpshooters present |
| Oregon | VERY LOW | Winter temps too cold |

### Pest Considerations

| Pest | Distribution | Risk Factors |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| **Glassy-Winged Sharpshooter** | Southern CA (established); spreading north | Vectors Pierce's Disease; flies farther than native sharpshooters |
| **Vine Mealybug** | 17+ CA counties including all major areas | Vectors leafroll virus; 4-7 generations/year |
| **Phylloxera** | Heavy clay soils: HIGH risk; Sandy soils: LOW risk | Only control: resistant rootstocks |
| **Spider Mites** | Highest late July-August in hot/dry conditions | Water stress increases vine susceptibility |
| **Nematodes** | Variable by soil type and region | X. index vectors fanleaf virus |

### Phylloxera Risk by Soil Type

| Soil Type | Risk Level | Reason |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| Heavy clay | HIGH | Napa, Sonoma, Lake, Mendocino, Monterey |
| Sandy soils | LOW | Higher silicon content; temps >32°C deter phylloxera |
| San Joaquin Valley | MODERATE | Present but damage less severe |

### Marine Cooling Effect Quantified

| Comparison | Temperature Difference |
|------------|----------------------|
| Southern Napa (Carneros) vs Northern Napa (St. Helena+) | 15°F (80°F vs 95°F avg daytime) |
| Petaluma Gap vs nearby inland | Up to 15°F cooler |

### Diurnal Temperature Variation by Region

| Region | Day-Night Swing | Classification |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Lake County | 50+ degrees | Extreme |
| Paso Robles | 50+ degrees | Extreme |
| Santa Ynez Valley | Up to 70°F | Extreme (recorded) |
| Petaluma Gap | 40-50 degrees | High |
| Lodi | 35-40 degrees | Moderate-High |
| Napa Valley floor | 30-40 degrees | Moderate |
| Carneros | 30-40 degrees | Moderate |
| West Sonoma Coast | ~20 degrees | Low (ocean moderation) |

### Diurnal Range Viticultural Effects

| Effect | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Acid retention | Cool nights slow malic acid respiration |
| Sugar/acid balance | Warm days = sugar; cool nights = acid preservation |
| Phenolic development | Cool nights continue tannin/flavor synthesis at lower sugar |
| Color development | Temperature drops enhance anthocyanin accumulation |
| Heat recovery | Cool nights allow vines to recover from daytime heat stress |

### Wind Patterns and Effects

| Corridor | Direction | Speed | Primary Effect |
|----------|-----------|-------|----------------|
| Petaluma Gap | West to east | 8-20 mph afternoon | 40-50°F diurnal swing; thicker grape skins |
| Van Duzer (Oregon) | Pacific to Willamette | Strong afternoon | Coolest Willamette AVA; extended ripening |
| Salinas Valley | Monterey Bay inland | Strong afternoon | "Wind tunnel" - slows ripening |
| Delta Breeze | Bay Area to Central Valley | Moderate | Enables Mediterranean climate in Lodi |

### Wind Effects on Viticulture

| Effect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Disease pressure | REDUCES - canopy drying reduces Botrytis, mildew |
| Grape skins | Thicker cuticles from wind stress = disease resistance |
| Yields | Smaller but more concentrated |
| Ripening | Later - extended hang time |
| Spray programs | Fewer sprays often needed in windy areas |

### Rain Shadow Effects

| Region | Mountain Range | Effect |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Napa Valley | Mayacamas (west) | Lessens Pacific storm impact |
| Willamette Valley | Coast Range | Most rain falls in mountains before reaching valley |
| Yamhill-Carlton AVA | Coast Range (3,500 ft) | Protected from heavy rainfall |
| Eastern Oregon/Columbia Valley | Cascades | Dramatically drier - warm climate wines |

### Elevation Effects

| Factor | Rule of Thumb |
|--------|---------------|
| Temperature lapse rate | 3.3-5°F cooler per 1,000 ft elevation |
| Dry air lapse rate | ~5°F per 1,000 ft |
| Moist air lapse rate | ~3.3°F per 1,000 ft |
| Fog line (Napa/Sonoma) | 1,600-1,800 feet |
| Above fog line | More direct sun; earlier warming; can be WARMER than valley |

### Elevation and Frost Risk

| Elevation Zone | Frost Risk | Reason |
|----------------|-----------|--------|
| Valley floor | HIGH | Cold air pools at lowest points |
| Mid-slope (200-800 ft) | MODERATE | Some drainage, some pooling |
| Upper hillside (800+ ft) | LOWER | Above cold air pool |
| Mountain top | VARIABLE | Exposed but no cold air pooling |

- GDD[°F] = 9/5 × GDD[°C]

| Threshold | °F | °C | Application |
|-----------|----|----|-------------|
| Frost damage (green tissue) | 28-32 | -2 to 0 | Frost protection trigger |
| Photosynthesis decline | >95 | >35 | Heat stress begins |
| Powdery mildew suppression | >95 (12+ hrs) | >35 | Disease management |
| Berry sunburn | >113 | >45 | Canopy management trigger |
